project idea

Web portal for Australian farmers to review profiles of potential fruit picker labours from the Pacific Island like Tonga, Vanuatu, Fiji, Samoa, and others. Farmers will post their labour requirements and labours applicant can apply to the farmers posting. The portal will be web based and easily accessible via a web browser. It will also be available in Android and Apple app stores.

Currently, individuals from Australia with ties to the Pacific islands, travel back to their home country to recruit fruit pick labours to bring back to Australian farmers. Sometimes the Pacific Island govt will organize a group to be sent to Australia. The hard working and able candidates are sometimes left behind, instead they sent their relatives which are not very active and struggle to do prolong labour work like fruit picking. By connecting the labour force to the Australian farmers enable access to the best labour force available.

This is a portal where Australian farmers can register their interest to hire fruit pick labours from the Pacific Islands to pick their fruits like mango, grapes, oranges etc at their farm during the harvesting season. The portal will be accessible with a web browser or downloadable from Android and Apple app store. Each Pacific island country will access a version of the portal in their own language, their country ip address will trigger the portal to display the application portal in their local language.  They will also have the option to select the English language if they want.   The public version will not have any verification of the fruit pickers details while the paid premium version will have verification of the fruit picker, health, weight, height, education background, fruit picker experience, ability to speak English and other relevant information. Later, a full service offering where the portal agency screen, recruit and deliver the fruit pickers to the farmers place of business. The initial applicant information will be provided by the applicant and verified by the portal staff through face to face or video call. This will confirm whether they are fit to carry out fruit picking in Australia, have the necessary documentation and clearance to travel from their country to Australia.  For the premium option, the farmers will pay a finder’s fee or hourly rate for each labour work supplied by the portal agency. Farmers will have to be verified for business standard, history, fruits they are harvesting, ability to house and feed the labours during the harvesting season, ability to pay the rates agreed and adhere to Australian labour law, manage security and safety of the labours on and off the field.  Once established, seek to create an industry standard certification that is acceptable to Australia farmers labour requirements. The certification will help to build a premium on labour candidates being vetted through the portal application process. This will set the portal candidates above other source of labour force from the Pacific islands and fend off competitors or newcomers.

This project for the web portal creation will require a web hosting and we can start off on a shared hosting. As the company grow then we can consider having a dedicated server for web hosting. We will also need an Australian domain registration together with an SSL certificate to secure the portal contents for website visitors and email service to go with the new domain name. The actual application can be an off the shelf application as it is cheaper and does not require a developer upfront, but a purpose build portal will require a web developer to design, build the website and add additional functionality. 

If we choose to go with a customized web portal, then we will need a web developer to build the web portal as well as a mobile Android/iOS developer to format the portal and submit to Android app store and Apple app store. We will also need a marketing personnel to manage the website keywords optimization, search engine marketing, social media and traditional media like newspaper, radio, tv etc. The premium service will require personnel with local knowledge of each labour market to do the verification of the labour candidates.  We will also need sales personnel to established clientele in Australia fruit farmers.

A successful implementation of this project will address the issue that Australian farmers are facing today with the shortage of labour force to pick the fruits during the farm harvesting season. It will also mean a better labour market with the eager and willing candidates to carry out the work during the harvesting season. Australia will also inject some needed cash into these Pacific Island economies through the paid wages of these fruit pickers. This is a win-win for the Australian government improving ties and influence in the South Pacific, farmers getting the needed labours for their harvest and these labours from the Pacific Islands injecting funds into their local communities.
